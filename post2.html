My Journey into Programming

November 12, 2025 · 4 min read

Everyone's path into programming is different. Some people discover it in childhood, others find it later in life. My journey started with curiosity and evolved into a passion that changed my career and my life.

The Spark

It all started when I wanted to build a simple website. I had an idea, but no clue how to bring it to life. So I did what anyone would do - I searched "how to make a website" and fell down the rabbit hole.

That first "Hello World" on a webpage was magical. It was simple, even trivial, but it was mine. I had created something from nothing, just by typing some text into a file. That feeling of creation, of bringing ideas to life through code, hooked me instantly.

The Struggle

Let's be honest - the beginning was tough. I made every mistake possible:

  • Forgetting semicolons and spending hours debugging
  • Not understanding the difference between = and ==
  • Copy-pasting code without understanding what it did
  • Giving up on tutorials halfway through
  • Feeling like everyone else "got it" faster than me

There were moments when I wanted to quit. When nothing worked and error messages seemed to be written in a foreign language. When I spent an entire day on a problem only to realize I was missing a closing bracket.

"The only way to learn a new programming language is by writing programs in it." - Dennis Ritchie

The Breakthrough

Everything changed when I stopped trying to learn everything at once and started building things. Small things. Imperfect things. Things that barely worked but were mine.

My first real project was a to-do list app. Revolutionary? No. But it taught me more than any tutorial ever could. I learned about:

  • DOM manipulation and event handling
  • Storing data (even just in the browser)
  • Debugging real problems, not tutorial exercises
  • The satisfaction of solving your own problems

What I Wish I Knew Then

Looking back, here's what I would tell my younger self:

1. Confusion is Normal

Everyone feels lost at the beginning. That frustration you feel? It's not a sign you're bad at this - it's a sign you're learning. The difference between beginners and experienced developers isn't that experienced devs don't get confused; it's that they're comfortable being confused.

2. Build Things, Don't Just Learn

Tutorial hell is real. You can watch a hundred videos, but until you build something yourself, you haven't really learned. Start with tiny projects and gradually make them more complex.

3. Google is Your Best Friend

Professional developers Google things constantly. It's not cheating - it's how the job works. Learn to search effectively, read documentation, and adapt solutions to your needs.

4. Community Matters

Join communities, ask questions, share your work. The programming community can be incredibly supportive. Don't be afraid to show your beginner projects - everyone started somewhere.

Where I Am Now

Today, I'm still learning. I probably always will be - that's the nature of programming. But now I'm comfortable with not knowing everything. I know how to figure things out, where to look for help, and most importantly, I know that every expert was once a beginner who didn't give up.

If you're just starting your programming journey, my advice is simple: start small, be patient with yourself, and keep building. The path isn't always linear, but if you keep at it, you'll be amazed at what you can create.

This blog is part of that journey. A place to document what I learn, share what I build, and hopefully help others who are walking the same path.
